Honey Glazed Halloumi

This honey glazed halloumi is sliced, marinated in a tangy, flavorful honey sesame sauce, then browned quickly in a skillet. Greek in origin, and with a high melting point, this is classic fried halloumi with a twist.

Ingredients

  • 1 (7 ounce) block halloumi cheese
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon freshly-squeezed lime juice
  • 2 teaspoons dried minced garlic (such as McCormickÂ®)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili pepper flakes, or to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • fresh lime wedges

Steps

  1. Slice halloumi lengthwise into 6 equal slices and pat dry with a paper towel. Add halloumi slices to a shallow container
  2. For glaze, combine honey, sesame oil, lime juice, minced garlic, sesame seeds, and chili flakes in a small bowl. Spoon glaze over halloumi, cover, and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour or up to overnight.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Place halloumi in the pan, leaving plenty of space between slices. Cook until slices are a nice golden brown, 1 to 2 minutes per side. They will brown quickly.
  4. Lower the heat to a simmer, add any remaining glaze to the skillet to quickly warm up. Spoon glaze over halloumi until well coated.
  5. Place halloumi slices on a plate with some fresh lime wedges, drizzle with any leftover glaze in the skillet, and serve immediately.
